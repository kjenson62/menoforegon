Why "Men of Oregon"

The story behind the name.

March 12, 2026 · Kevin Jenson

Bill Bowerman didn't just coach runners. He built men. At the University of Oregon, his track program became legendary not only for its wins but for the kind of people it produced — men who understood that the point of competition wasn't the medal but the forging.

His most famous line captures it:

“The cowards never started and the weak died along the way. That leaves us.”

There's nothing gentle about those words. They're not meant to comfort. They're meant to separate — to draw a line between people who talk about doing hard things and people who actually do them. Between those who start and those who merely consider it. Between those who endure and those who quit when endurance becomes the only option.

Why It Still Matters

We live in a time that's very good at offering shortcuts. Optimized routines, productivity hacks, passive income, life on easy mode. And there's nothing wrong with efficiency. But somewhere in the optimization, we've lost the thing Bowerman understood instinctively: that the struggle is the point. That you can't shortcut your way to character.

Men of Oregon is an attempt to take that idea seriously — not as nostalgia for some rugged past, but as a living philosophy. The forests are still here. The mountains still demand something of you when you climb them. And the questions about how to live a good life haven't gotten any easier just because we have better tools.

What This Space Is For

This blog will be a place for thinking out loud. About ethics, about what we owe each other, about the kind of men we want to be and the kind of communities we want to build. It'll be personal sometimes and philosophical other times. It'll be rooted in Oregon — its landscape, its history, its particular stubborn spirit — but it won't be limited to it.

If you're someone who believes that becoming a better person is a daily practice and not a destination, you'll find something here.

The cowards never started. You're reading this, so you did. Welcome.
